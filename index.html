Mapping Affordable, Safe, and Transit-Accessible Housing to UW

A smart interactive geography dashboard exploring spatial patterns, inequality, environment, mobility, or public data through maps and charts.

Research Question

How are geographic patterns distributed across the study area, and what do these patterns reveal about social or environmental inequality?

Study Area

This project focuses on a selected city, region, neighborhood, or community using spatial data and interactive visualization.

Why It Matters

The dashboard helps viewers understand spatial differences more clearly by connecting maps, data, and real-world geographic questions.

Methods and Data

This project uses public geographic data, cleaned and organized for interactive visualization. The dashboard combines map-based analysis, filters, and charts to help viewers explore spatial patterns. Possible methods include data cleaning, classification, spatial comparison, choropleth mapping, point mapping, and visual storytelling.
